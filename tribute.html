Louie Zamperini

A moment of pain is worth a lifetime glory

Here's a time line of Louie Zamperini :

  • Louie was born in Olean, New York to Anthony and Louise Zamperini on January 26th, 1917.

  • Freshman year, Louie starts to track and field as a sport with the help of his older brother. Louie gets devoted to running.

  • April of 1936 he starts to train for the Olympics.

  • Louie Zamperini ran in the 1936 Olympics. He was one of few American runners in the 5000m event. He finished 8th place as 19 year old.

  • Later he enlists in the Air Force to fight for America in WWII. He becomes the bombadier.

  • Louie was assigned into air corps, and was the bombardier in the B-24 plane. He meets his friend Phil.

  • On May 27th, 1943 Louie's plane crashed in the ocean.

  • Louie and two other crew mates survive the crash of their B-24 in the Pacific. They survive on a life raft for the next 47 days, with little food and water.

  • Stuck with two small rafts they were fighting for their survival from sharks, starvation, and the sun.

  • Mac eventually died to deprivation and illness and was buried at sea.

  • Louie and one other crew member make it to land. They are then captured and sent off to Japanese POW camps.

  • He spent two years being starved, beaten, and tortured by the Japanese. He was released when American soldiers came to rescue them after winning the war.

  • Louie suffers from PTSD from the POW camp. He turns to alcohol and becomes addicted. He said once That "The alcohol brought him a pleasant numbness".

  • Later he attends a Billy Graham sermon. There he turns his life around, and towards God. He quits being an alcoholic and works on forgivness.

  • Zamperini was a torchbearer for the 1984 Summer Olympic Games in Los Angeles and the 1998 Winter Games in Nagano.

  • In October 2008, Zamperini was inducted into the National Italian American Sports Hall of Fame in Chicago, Illinois.

  • Louie Zamperini sadly died in July of 2014. He died of Pnemonia at age 97.
